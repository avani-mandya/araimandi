Why this matters

Precision posture,
injury prevention

In Bharatanatyam, Araimandi is the foundational half-sitting stance. Incorrect alignment causes lasting knee and lower back damage over time.

01

Knees must open outward ~90°

Both knees should point directly sideways — not forward. The analyzer flags any deviation in real-time.

02

Spine stays perfectly vertical

Any forward or backward lean increases load on the lumbar spine and disrupts balance.

03

Hips square and wide

The pelvis should be square to the front with hip width indicating a proper wide stance.
