Why this matters

Precision posture,
injury prevention

In Bharatanatyam, Araimandi is the foundational half-sitting stance. Incorrect alignment — knees forward instead of outward, an arched back, or flat feet — causes lasting knee and lower back damage over time.

01

Knees must open outward ~90°

The knee angle relative to the hip joint determines correct rotation. Both knees should point directly sideways, not forward.

02

Spine stays perfectly vertical

Any forward or backward lean increases compressive load on the lumbar spine and disrupts the balance of the posture.

03

Hips square and wide

The pelvis should be square to the front and the hip width relative to shoulders should indicate a proper wide stance.
